## Chicken and Vegetable Stir-fry

This recipe is quick, versatile, and packed with flavor and nutrients. It's perfect for a weeknight meal.

### Why Stir-fry?

Stir-frying is a traditional Chinese cooking technique that involves quickly cooking ingredients in a small amount of very hot oil while continuously stirring them. It's an excellent method because:

*   **Speed:** High heat and constant movement mean food cooks very quickly.
*   **Nutrient Retention:** The short cooking time helps to preserve the vitamins and minerals in vegetables, keeping them crisp and vibrant.
*   **Flavor Development:** The intense heat creates a "wok hei" (breath of the wok) – a smoky, caramelized flavor unique to stir-frying.
*   **Versatility:** You can use almost any combination of proteins and vegetables, making it easy to adapt to what you have on hand.
*   **Minimal Oil:** It uses relatively little oil compared to other frying methods.

### Ingredients

**For the Chicken:**
*   1 lb (about 450g) boneless, skinless chicken breast or thigh, cut into 1-inch pieces
*   1 tbsp soy sauce
*   1 tsp cornstarch (or arrowroot powder)
*   ½ tsp sesame oil (optional, for flavor)
*   Pinch of white pepper

**For the Sauce:**
*   ¼ cup soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
*   2 tbsp oyster sauce (or vegetarian stir-fry sauce)
*   1 tbsp rice vinegar
*   1 tbsp brown sugar (or honey/maple syrup)
*   1 tsp cornstarch
*   2 tbsp water or chicken broth
*   (Optional: ¼ tsp red pepper flakes for heat)

**For the Vegetables & Aromatics:**
*   2 tbsp cooking oil (vegetable, canola, or peanut oil)
*   1 large onion, thinly sliced or chopped
*   2 cloves garlic, minced
*   1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated or minced
*   4-5 cups mixed stir-fry vegetables, cut into bite-sized pieces (e.g., broccoli florets, bell peppers (any color), carrots, snap peas, mushrooms, baby corn, bok choy)

**For Serving:**
*   Cooked rice or noodles
*   Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)
*   Chopped green onions (optional, for garnish)

### Equipment

*   Large wok or a large, deep skillet
*   Large mixing bowls
*   Whisk
*   Cutting board and sharp knife

### Instructions

1.  **Marinate the Chicken:** In a medium bowl, combine the chicken pieces with 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp cornstarch, ½ tsp sesame oil (if using), and a pinch of white pepper. Toss to coat evenly. Let it marinate for at least 10-15 minutes while you prepare other ingredients.
2.  **Prepare the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together all sauce ingredients: ¼ cup soy sauce, 2 tbsp oyster sauce, 1 tbsp rice vinegar, 1 tbsp brown sugar, 1 tsp cornstarch, and 2 tbsp water/broth. Set aside.
3.  **Heat the Wok:** Heat 1 tbsp of cooking oil in your wok or large skillet over high heat until shimmering and almost smoking.
4.  **Cook the Chicken:** Add the marinated chicken to the hot wok in a single layer (you might need to do this in batches if your wok isn't large enough to avoid overcrowding). Sear for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Remove the cooked chicken from the wok and set aside.
5.  **Sauté Aromatics & Vegetables:** Add the remaining 1 tbsp of cooking oil to the wok. Add the sliced onion and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until slightly softened. Add the minced garlic and ginger, stir-fry for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
6.  **Add Vegetables:** Add the harder vegetables first (like broccoli and carrots). Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes. Then add softer vegetables (like bell peppers, snap peas, mushrooms, baby corn, bok choy). Continue to stir-fry for another 3-5 minutes, until vegetables are crisp-tender but still have a slight bite.
7.  **Combine and Sauce:** Return the cooked chicken to the wok with the vegetables. Give the prepared sauce a quick whisk again (cornstarch tends to settle) and pour it over the chicken and vegetables.
8.  **Thicken the Sauce:** Stir continuously for 1-2 minutes as the sauce simmers and thickens, coating all the ingredients evenly.
9.  **Serve:** Remove from heat immediately. Serve hot over cooked rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions, if desired.

### Tips for Success

*   **Mise en Place:** Have all your ingredients prepped and ready before you start cooking. Stir-frying is fast, so you won't have time to chop or measure during the process.
*   **Don't Overcrowd:** Cook in batches if necessary to ensure ingredients sear rather than steam. Overcrowding lowers the wok's temperature.
*   **High Heat:** Maintain high heat throughout the stir-frying process. This is key to achieving that signature crisp-tender texture and smoky flavor.
*   **Cut Evenly:** Cut all ingredients into similar-sized pieces for even cooking.
*   **Customize:** Feel free to swap chicken for beef, pork, shrimp, or tofu. Use your favorite vegetables!

Enjoy your homemade Chicken and Vegetable Stir-fry!
